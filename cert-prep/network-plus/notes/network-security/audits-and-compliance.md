## Audits and Compliance

Audits and compliance ensure that an organization's network and data handling follow regulatory standards and security best practices. Key topics include:

---

### Data Locality
- **Definition:** The physical location where data is stored and processed.
- **Importance:** Legal and regulatory requirements may dictate that certain data (e.g., customer information) must remain within a specific country or region.
- **Network Impact:** Network administrators must consider the location of servers, cloud providers, and backups to ensure compliance.

### PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)
- **Definition:** A set of security standards designed to protect credit card information.
- **Requirements Include:**
  - Secure network architecture (firewalls, segmentation)
  - Strong access control measures
  - Encryption of cardholder data in transit and at rest
  - Regular vulnerability testing and monitoring
- **Network Considerations:** Ensuring secure communication channels, proper authentication, and intrusion detection systems.

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
- **Definition:** European Union regulation protecting personal data and privacy of EU citizens.
- **Key Points:**
  - Data collection must be lawful, transparent, and for specific purposes
  - Individuals have the right to access, correct, or delete their data
  - Organizations must implement technical and organizational measures to protect personal data
- **Network Considerations:** Implementing secure storage, proper logging, access controls, and secure data transfers.
