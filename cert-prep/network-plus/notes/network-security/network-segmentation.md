# Network Segmentation

**Network segmentation** is the practice of dividing a network into smaller, isolated segments to improve security, performance, and manageability. Each segment can have its own policies, access controls, and monitoring, which helps reduce risk and contain potential breaches.

---

## Key Segments

### 1. IoT (Internet of Things)
- Devices: Smart home devices, sensors, cameras, wearables.
- Purpose: Isolate IoT devices from the main network because they often have weaker security.
- Benefit: Limits exposure if an IoT device is compromised.

### 2. IIoT (Industrial Internet of Things)
- Devices: Industrial sensors, robotic machinery, smart meters.
- Purpose: Separate industrial devices from IT networks to ensure operational continuity.
- Benefit: Protects critical industrial operations from cyber threats.

### 3. SCADA (Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition)
- Devices: Systems that monitor and control industrial processes remotely.
- Purpose: Isolate SCADA networks to secure critical infrastructure.
- Benefit: Prevents unauthorized access to control systems.

### 4. ICS (Industrial Control Systems)
- Devices: PLCs (Programmable Logic Controllers), HMIs (Human-Machine Interfaces).
- Purpose: Dedicated network segment for operational systems.
- Benefit: Reduces risk of industrial disruption due to network attacks.

### 5. OT (Operational Technology)
- Scope: Encompasses ICS and IIoT systems.
- Purpose: Separate OT from IT to protect operational assets and processes.
- Benefit: Ensures industrial processes run safely without interference from IT traffic.

### 6. Guest Network
- Devices: Visitor laptops, phones, tablets.
- Purpose: Provide Internet access without granting access to internal corporate resources.
- Benefit: Prevents guests from reaching sensitive systems while allowing connectivity.

### 7. BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)
- Devices: Employee personal laptops, smartphones, tablets.
- Purpose: Isolate personal devices from critical corporate networks.
- Benefit: Limits malware or data leakage from personal devices affecting the corporate environment.

## Benefits of Network Segmentation
- **Improved security:** Limits the spread of malware and unauthorized access.
- **Better performance:** Reduces congestion by controlling traffic flow.
- **Simplified management:** Easier to enforce policies for specific groups of devices.
- **Compliance:** Helps meet regulatory requirements for sensitive data protection.
